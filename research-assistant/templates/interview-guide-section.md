# Interview Guide Section Template (1:1 Interviews)

Embed this inside Section 6 of the research plan doc. For 60-minute semi-structured interviews focused on user research (Mom Test rules apply throughout).

## Discussion Guide — [Study name]

**Format:** Semi-structured 1:1 interview · 60 min · [In-person / Remote]
**Mom Test compliance:** All core questions are past-behaviour anchored. Hypotheticals appear only in the optional reveal section at the end.

---

### Pre-session checklist (moderator)

- [ ] Recording consent obtained (verbal + written)
- [ ] Participant has water / can break
- [ ] Notetaker on standby; recording started
- [ ] Phone silenced; notification mode active
- [ ] Discussion guide visible but not in participant's eyeline

---

### Section 1 — Warm-up (5–10 min)

**Goal:** Build rapport. Lower cortisol. Establish that this is a conversation, not a test.

Suggested opens (pick one — don't read all):
- "Thanks for joining. Before we get into anything specific, can you tell me a bit about your day so far?"
- "How long have you been using [Carousell / similar marketplace]? What got you started?"
- "Walk me through your typical week — when do you usually pick up your phone for personal stuff?"

**Stop signal:** Move on once participant gives multi-sentence answers and looks settled. Do not push further.

---

### Section 2 — Context and current behaviour (10 min)

**Goal:** Map the participant's current world without introducing anything we're researching.

Anchor every question in past behaviour. Pattern: "When was the last time…" / "Walk me through what happened" / "What did you do next?"

Sample questions (adapt to the study):
- "Tell me about the last time you [target activity, e.g. listed something to sell]. What were you trying to do?"
- "Walk me through what happened, step by step. What did you do first?"
- "What apps or tools did you use? Why those?"
- "What got in the way?"

**Avoid:** "How often do you…" (generic), "Do you usually…" (averages out specifics).

---

### Section 3 — Past-behaviour core (20–25 min)

**Goal:** Extract specific, fact-based behavioural data on the topic the study is about. This is where 80% of insight lives.

Structure as 2–3 sub-topics. For each sub-topic, follow this ladder:

1. **Anchor in a specific past instance:** "When was the last time [specific situation]?"
2. **Walk-through:** "Walk me through what happened. What did you do first?"
3. **Decision points:** "Why did you choose to do that and not [alternative]?"
4. **Friction:** "What was the most annoying part of that?"
5. **Workaround:** "What did you try before that worked / didn't work?"
6. **Outcome:** "How did it end?"

[Embed the study-specific sub-topics here. Each should have its own anchor question + ladder.]

Example sub-topic — "Pricing a listing":
- "Tell me about the last item you sold. How did you decide on the price?"
- "Walk me through how you arrived at that number."
- "Did you change the price after listing? Why?"
- "What else did you do — search comparable listings, ask someone, just guess?"
- "How did the final price compare to what you originally set?"

**Researcher discipline:**
- After every question, count to 3 in your head before adding anything.
- If they say "yeah, it was fine", that's not data — push for the specific. "Tell me about a time it wasn't fine."
- If they say "I usually do X", redirect: "Tell me about a specific recent time."

---

### Section 4 — Emotional deep-dive (10 min)

**Goal:** Surface emotional triggers, anxieties, and identity-level concerns that drive behaviour but rarely volunteer themselves.

Probes:
- "What's the most frustrating thing about this whole process?"
- "When you think about [topic], what worries you?"
- "Was there a time you felt embarrassed / anxious / proud about [topic]?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be — and what's actually getting in the way of fixing it today?"

**Listen for:** trust language, identity language ("I'm not the kind of person who…"), comparisons to other people ("most sellers I know…").

**Don't:** introduce concepts. Don't pitch solutions. Don't react visibly to negative emotions — let them land.

---

### Section 5 — Optional concept reveal (5 min, only if needed)

**Use only if the study explicitly requires concept reaction.**

If included:
- Introduce the concept once, briefly. Show, don't pitch.
- Ask: "What's the first thing that comes to mind?"
- Then: "What wouldn't work for you here?"
- Then: "If you tried this and it didn't help, what would have caused that?"

**Validity caveat to put in synthesis:** Reactions to concepts are stated, not behavioural. Do not treat positive reactions as predictive of adoption.

---

### Section 6 — Wrap (5 min)

**Goal:** Get referrals and follow-up consent. Close warmly.

- "Is there anything we didn't talk about that you think is important?"
- "Who else do you know who [target behaviour]? Would you be willing to introduce me?"
- "Can I follow up by message if I have a quick question later?"
- Thank, confirm incentive details, end recording.

---

## Moderator Reminders (don't print — for moderator only)

- Talk less. Aim for participant to be speaking 80%+ of the time.
- Silence is fine. Count to 3 before filling it.
- Never explain why you're asking. Never confirm "this is helpful, thanks!" mid-interview — it telegraphs hypotheses.
- Take light notes; let the notetaker do the heavy capture.
- After each session, debrief with notetaker for 10 min. Capture top 3 quotes, top 3 surprises.
