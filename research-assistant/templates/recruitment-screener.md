# Recruitment Screener Template

For external recruiters or in-house panel screening. The screener filters participants for the study — its job is to **filter on past behaviour, not telegraph the study topic**.

---

## Screener — [Study name]

**Purpose:** Recruit [N] participants matching [segment definition]
**Fielding partner:** [Panel / vendor / in-house]
**Incentive:** [Amount per market]
**Privacy:** [Data handling, retention, anonymisation]

---

## Critical Rules (Don't Break)

1. **Filter on past behaviour**, not just demographics. "Carousell user, 25–45" is useless. "Listed an item for sale in the last 30 days" is a recruitment criterion.
2. **Hide the study topic.** If your screener says "We're studying the new listing flow", fraudsters will claim listing experience to win the incentive. Phrase screener questions as general behaviour.
3. **Include attention checks** to filter speeders and bots.
4. **Quota-control** for segment balance — don't end up with 80% pro sellers if the study needs balanced casual/pro.
5. **Exclude** anyone who recently participated in our research (cool-down period 60–90 days).

## Screener Structure

### Section 1 — General intro (no study topic)

> "Thanks for your interest. We're looking for people to share their experiences with online shopping and selling. The session will take [N] minutes and you'll receive [incentive]. To see if you're a good fit, please answer a few questions."

### Section 2 — Behavioural filters

[Replace with study-specific behavioural questions. Ask about behaviour, not awareness.]

**Q1 — Recency (general behaviour)**
"In the last 30 days, which of the following have you done? (Select all that apply.)"
- [ ] Bought something secondhand online
- [ ] Sold something online
- [ ] Browsed online marketplaces without buying
- [ ] None of the above

[Filter logic: include only those who match the target behaviour. Phrase the target behaviour among others — don't isolate it.]

**Q2 — Frequency (specific to target)**
"In the last 90 days, roughly how many [target activity, e.g. items have you listed for sale]?"
- 0
- 1–2
- 3–5
- 6–10
- 11+

[Filter logic: usually 1+ for "active user"; 6+ for "frequent user".]

**Q3 — Category exposure (if relevant)**
"Which of the following have you ever bought or sold online? (Select all that apply.)"
- Electronics
- Fashion
- Home goods
- Cars or motorcycles
- Services (renovation, cleaning, etc.)
- None

**Q4 — Recency on Carousell (if Carousell-specific)**
"When was the last time you used the Carousell app or website?"
- In the last 7 days
- 8–30 days ago
- 31–90 days ago
- More than 90 days ago
- I've never used Carousell

[Don't reveal Carousell as the focus until necessary — phrase among other apps.]

### Section 3 — Demographics for quota balancing

- Market / region
- Age bracket
- Gender (if relevant — be inclusive)
- Language(s) spoken at home (for multi-language studies)

### Section 4 — Logistics

- Available during [fielding window]?
- Has a working [device] for [mode] sessions?
- Comfortable speaking [study language]?
- Has participated in any user research in the last 90 days? (Exclude if yes.)

### Section 5 — Attention check

Insert a simple attention check to catch speeders / bots:

> "To confirm you're reading carefully, please select 'Strongly disagree' for this question."
> [Likert 5: Strongly agree → Strongly disagree]

Exclude any participant who fails.

### Section 6 — Closing

> "Thanks for completing the screener. If you match, our team will contact you within [N] days to schedule. If you don't, we may invite you to future studies."

Do not reveal whether they matched. Don't show the topic.

## Quota Plan

| Segment | Target N | Inclusion | Exclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Casual seller] | [N] | Listed 1–5 items in 90 days | Pro seller status |
| [Pro seller] | [N] | Listed 10+ items in 90 days, has Pro badge | New seller (<6 months) |
| [Active buyer] | [N] | Bought 2+ items in 90 days | Has not bought in 90 days |
| [Lapsed user] | [N] | Used Carousell 1+ years ago, none in 90 days | Currently active |

Recruit to quota — don't over-recruit one segment.

---

## Screener Pitfalls To Avoid

- **Topic leakage** — "We're studying [feature]" tips off fraudsters. Keep it generic.
- **Demographic-only filter** — useless for behaviour-driven research.
- **No attention check** — bot/speeder pollution can be 20%+ of panel responses.
- **Vague language thresholds** — "fluent in English" is unreliable; use "comfortable answering open-ended questions in [language]".
- **Long screener** — over 10 questions inflates dropout. Keep it tight.
- **No re-participation cooldown** — same panel members across studies = professional respondents who've learned to game screeners.
